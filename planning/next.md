## v0.4 ‚Äî Curated onboarding (read-only) *(now)*

Strategic context: [`roadmap.md`](roadmap.md) ‚Üí *v0.4*. The barrier to adoption is the analyst's time: they won't re-enter on Vidit work they've already published to X. Two self-serve channels remove that cost: the analyst **uploads their official X archive** (full history, no API) and **tags a Vidit bot** on each new geolocation tweet. Machine-produced geolocations land as **`detected`** (visible everywhere but clearly marked) and become **`geolocated`** once the owner vouches; a direct submission or a request fulfilment is born `geolocated`.

Consent is the action itself: the upload and the tag are in-product and cover only the analyst's own posts; nothing is fetched, processed, or published for anyone who hasn't acted. Phases: **A** (extraction core + state machine + legal review), **B** (the on-ramp: backfill, bot, Detections queue, value layer), **C** (go public, gated on the legal review). Phase C can't open until `detected` renders marked on every surface.

**Coverage is text-only, a stated limitation.** On a 48.5k-tweet external OSINT corpus (853 analysts) the text parser recovers **~86 %** of geolocations at ~0 % false positives (decimal dominates; DMS / hemisphere are the handled long tail). The remaining **~14 %** put the coordinate only inside the image; recovering them would need vision over every backfilled media, **out of scope** (documented to the analyst; the OCR cross-check row is a *value* add, not a recovery path).

**Landed** (see [CHANGELOG](../CHANGELOG.md) ‚Üí *Unreleased*): the Phase A detection spine (acquire / stitch / detect bricks, `assemble`, the no-persist preview, `detected` marked on every surface, dev-fed by a synthetic archive via `make seed-detections`); the request + geolocation + detection merge into one `events` lifecycle (`requested` ‚Üí `detected` ‚Üí `geolocated` ‚Üí `closed`, the promotion apparatus removed, the `/bounties` **router** removed, request / geolocation / detection now filtered views over `GET /events`); the event-model refactor (`owner_id` split from the durable `event_geolocators` credit, `event_claims` ‚Üí `event_investigators`, `submit`/`reject` ‚Üí `geolocate`/`close`, `location` ‚Üí `event_coords` + a new `capture_source_coords`, the `proof_images` table dropped in favour of `media(role='proof')` uploaded at publish, stored IP/UA dropped); the design-system primitives audit; and the **honest source contract** (`source_url` / `source_posted_at` nullable + `ck_events_source_url_status`, the `source_url_required` gate at the `geolocate` promotion, the self-source fallback removed, archive video capture, see [`ingestion.md`](../docs/ingestion.md)). **What's left**, below: the **analyst on-ramp** (archive upload UI + tweets-only guard, the bot), the **ingest-contract test fixtures per typology** (`backend/tests/ingest_contract/`, in progress), and the **legal + release** gates. The synthetic archive is the dev-only feeder; the real analyst upload is the deferred on-ramp.

**Decision: no handle-ownership verification in v0.4.** X's OAuth consent screen is too broad even at the minimal `users.read` scope ("Any account you can view, including protected accounts") for a privacy-conscious OSINT audience, and X offers no lighter identity integration: no OpenID Connect, and OAuth 1.0a "Sign in with Twitter" is *worse* (timeline + follows + settings). So OAuth handle-ownership is dropped (see the closed *Continue with X* PR, kept on its branch if ever wanted). An import therefore attributes work to a `@handle` **without proving the uploader owns it**. The exposure (fabricating an archive to impersonate a handle) is high-effort and low-likelihood in closed beta, and everything lands as `detected` draft (nothing is publicly vouched without a later geolocate). **Ownership proof + a claim/dispute pipeline move to v0.5** (needed there regardless, e.g. a geolocation stolen and imported under the wrong handle).

## Refactors

Engineering-hygiene work, not gated on any version ‚Äî pick these up when they unblock the next feature or when the smell starts to bite. Order is rough priority.

| Area | Item | Why / how |

| Frontend | Modernise `useEffect` patterns to satisfy `react-hooks/set-state-in-effect` | Next 16's `eslint-config-next/core-web-vitals` preset adds `react-hooks/set-state-in-effect` (a new React Compiler-integrated rule) and 15 sites in the frontend trip it today ‚Äî every `setX(...)` call inside a `useEffect` body, mostly auth-gating and bootstrap-data-load flows. The Next 16 upgrade PR downgrades the rule from `error` to `warn` in [`frontend/eslint.config.mjs`](../frontend/eslint.config.mjs) so the build stays green; this row is the actual cleanup. **Cross-cutting rules:**

- **Gamification stays separate from trust.** Scores, badges, and streaks must never influence the `trusted_contributor` flag and must never appear in the same UI surface as the trust filter. The platform's quality model rests on admin-curated trust + per-action moderation, not reputation math (see [`roadmap.md`](roadmap.md) ‚Üí *Future considerations ‚Üí Trust + governance*).
